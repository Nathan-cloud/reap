Securing a Promising Fututre of Rwanda

Fostering Primary Prevention

REAP believes that the success of children and the quality of their education and the strength of a community depend upon each other in order to break the cycle of poverty and generate opportunities for lifelong learning.

Advancing Literacy

REAP’s learner-centered centered approach to literacy development engages children in hands-on learning that heightens critical thinking, social emotional competencies, and dynamic reading and writing connections across subject areas.

What reap wants

REAP strives to address obstacles impeding Rwandan families’ ability to overcome socio-economic burdens that not only limit children’s educational opportunities, but also lead to a debilitating cycle of poverty that keeps generations of Rwandans disenfranchised and educationally disempowered, and thus unable to enhance their well-being..
